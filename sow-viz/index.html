Domain Foundation · Actual Flow Understanding

How NSR actually works

Distilled from the discovery call — the original domain understanding behind the SOW. Read this first if you're new to the project. Every concept in the Pipeline, Modules, and Sample Flows tabs maps back to one of the artifacts below.

A · Atomic Unit

The Buy/Sell — one load, three actors

NSR is a recycling company. Their entire business is captured in one transaction type: a Buy/Sell (B/S). It's a single load of recyclable material moving from a supplier through a hauler to an outlet. CieTrade records it; everything downstream — ledgers, invoices, postings, payments — derives from this one event.

Transports the load. Bills NSR for the move + fuel + dump charges.
NSR → pays them

Receives the material — the company NSR sells to.
→ pays NSR
It's a transaction. Basically it's a load which gets picked up from one supplier and transferred from the supplier to the outlet with the help of a hauler. That's all. The name for recording this transaction is BuySell in the ERP platform. — Discovery transcript, speaker_1
B · The Stack

Three systems, one business

NSR's stack revolves around three platforms. Knowing what each one does — and what they don't — is the key to understanding why this SOW exists. CieTrade is source of truth, Sage is the books, and iRadius is the automation glue currently keeping them aligned. TechJays is being asked to replace iRadius's job with something better.

"CTrade"

C · Data Hierarchy

Inside CieTrade — counterparty to invoice

Everything in CieTrade rolls up from master data. A Buy/Sell uses counterparty + order data to produce ledgers; ledgers get aggregated into invoices; invoices post out to Sage. Critically, the relationship from buy/sell to ledger is one-to-many, and ledger to invoice is many-to-one. That's where the matching complexity comes from.

D · The Hard Part

Three complexity levels of invoice matching

When a vendor invoice comes in, the system has to match its line items back to NSR's Buy/Sells. The shape of that match falls into three patterns — Level 3 is what makes this project hard.

L1 · DIRECT
1 Buy/Sell → 1 Invoice
Vendor sends one invoice for one B/S. Direct lookup by B/S number on the invoice. Solved by §4.1.1 in the SOW.
B/S 10342 → Invoice 12-32987
Easy
L2 · LINE-BY-LINE
N Buy/Sells → N Invoice lines
One invoice covers multiple loads, each as its own line. Each invoice line maps cleanly to one B/S. The SOW's 5-step line-match handles this (§4.2.1).
B/S 10342 → Line 1
B/S 10343 → Line 2
B/S 10344 → Line 3
Easy-medium
L3 · GROUPED
N Buy/Sells → 1 Invoice line
Vendor groups multiple B/Ss at the product level over a date range into a single line. To match, the system has to enumerate all candidate B/Ss in the period and find the subset whose sum equals the invoice line.
B/S 10342 B/S 10343 B/S 10344 → Line 1 (sum)
B/S 10345 B/S 10346 → Line 2 (sum)
Hard
One is a direct match with a buy/sell. Two is one invoice line matching with one buy/sell. The next complexity is multiple buy/sell transactions — ledgers are combined in a single invoice line. So there are three complexities like this. One and two are easy to solve. The third is kind of hard… only if we take and group all the buy/sells within that duration can we do it. — Discovery transcript, on matching

G · The Three Flavors

AP, AR, and Create Pay

Same buy/sell, same posting infrastructure, but three different posting "directions". Phase 1 of this project is AP only; AR is Phase 2. Create Pay sits between them — it's a hybrid month-end batch.

AP
Accounts Payable
NSR pays a vendor (a supplier or hauler).
  • Vendor sends NSR an invoice (email · upload · portal)
  • System extracts fields · matches to a B/S
  • Posts to Sage to record the obligation
  • NSR pays via check · ACH · EFT
  • iRadius confirms paid status back
money flow · NSR → vendor
AR
Accounts Receivable
A customer (the outlet) pays NSR. Phase 2.
  • NSR generates the invoice in CieTrade
  • Sends it to the customer (email or mail)
  • JPMC lockbox / EDI 820 captures the remittance
  • iRadius applies cash to invoices
  • Unapplied cash, deductions, disputes routed
money flow · customer → NSR
CP
Create Pay
Month-end batch for CP-terms counterparties (CP15, CP30, CP45). Hybrid.
  • Triggered on biz day 10–11 after period close
  • Net positive row → AP invoice (NSR pays them)
  • Net negative row → AR invoice (they pay NSR)
  • Same batch, mixed direction
  • Atomic post · locked after period close
money flow · both directions
Create Pay AP terms — when it is net positive, then it is AP invoice. When net is negative, then it is AR invoice that is put. So it's completely related to AP. — Discovery transcript, clarifying Create Pay scope
H · The Replacement

What iRadius does today — and what we're replacing

iRadius is an automation layer NSR added on top of Sage to avoid manually reconciling every invoice. Today, iRadius can't match every invoice cleanly because it doesn't know how to handle all of NSR's invoice shapes — particularly the L3 grouped case. That's the gap TechJays is filling.

AP · AR
Fully Paid
Payment matched to invoice; full amount applied; ledger balanced.
AP · AR
Partially Paid
Some money applied, but invoice total not covered. Left open.
AP · AR
Unpaid
Posted but no payment received yet. The aging clock is running.
AR ONLY
Unapplied Cash
Money received but no clear invoice match. Sits in unapplied bucket until disposed.
AR ONLY
DMS / Disputes
Deductions, short-pays, and customer disputes routed to AM or AR for resolution.
Sage is an accounting system which is purely manual. It's a big process — does a person sit and check whether this is completely paid or not? iRadius is the automation. So for me, everything is an end-of-day invoice. iRadius helps to clarify whether my invoices have been paid or not. — Discovery transcript, on why iRadius exists
I · Glossary

Terms you'll see across the docs

Quick reference for the domain-specific terms that show up in the SOW, transcripts, and sample invoices.

Buy/Sell B/S · BS
A single load of recyclable material moving from supplier → hauler → outlet. The atomic transaction unit in CieTrade. Every cost, revenue, ledger, and invoice traces back to a B/S.
Counterparty CPID
Any third party NSR transacts with — supplier, hauler, or outlet. Identified by CPID. Has address, billing terms, IsRemit flag, IsActive flag.
Order
A rate negotiation. Defines what NSR will pay (or charge) per unit of a material for a duration of time. Sets the price baseline that ledgers compute against.
Ledger
Per-party financial record generated from a B/S. One B/S can produce 2–3+ ledgers (one per party involved). Ledgers get aggregated into invoices.
Grade lines
Material classification on the B/S. Tied to expenses (hauler/outlet costs). Different grades have different pricing per the order.
CieTrade CTrade · CTR
The ERP NSR runs operations on. All B/Ss, ledgers, and invoice generation happen here.
Sage
Accounting platform. Receives invoice postings, tracks money in/out, supports tax calculation. Filled by SFTP feed + manual entries.
iRadius HighRadius · IRDS
Current automation between CieTrade and Sage. Reads payment files, matches to invoices, writes status back. Being augmented/replaced for AP coverage by this project.
JPMC
JPMorgan Chase — NSR's bank. Drops customer remittance files (lockbox ZIPs, EDI 820) into SFTP for the AR side (Phase 2).
AP Batch batch_id = invoice #
A group of ledgers posted together as one accounting record. Batch ID and invoice number are the same value.
Posting
The act of writing a batch to SFTP for downstream systems (Sage / iRadius) to consume. Two cadences: AP outbound twice daily, response inbound daily ~09:00.
Create Pay CP-terms · CP15 · CP30 · CP45
Month-end invoice generation for counterparties on CP payment terms. Net positive → AP, net negative → AR. Atomic batch at biz day 10–11.
DND
Discovery & Design — the formal scoping phase that comes after SOW signoff and before implementation. Where final scope is locked.
DMS
AR-side terminology for deduction management state. Used by iRadius for tracking customer disputes / short pays.
Unapplied Cash
AR-side: money received with no clear invoice to apply it against. Sits in a holding bucket until reconciled.

MODULE 01 · INGESTION T + 0 to T + 60s
Homewood's billing system emails the invoice PDF to APayable@nsrecycle.com. Within a minute, the MS Graph email monitor polls, sees the new message, and runs three invariants before accepting it:
  • Unread-preservation — read without marking read, so AP humans sharing the inbox still see it as new.
  • Forwarding-chain check — trace the chain; use the original sender (Homewood), not a forwarder, for vendor identity.
  • SHA-256 dedupe — hash the PDF bytes against the 30-day dedupe table to reject accidental re-forwards.
A new invoice record is created: UUID abc123…, source = email.

Azure Document Intelligence runs per-page with bounding boxes retained so the UI can highlight fields on the PDF when a user clicks them. The header extractor returns the canonical 27 fields; every field carries an independent confidence score.

Vendor resolution → CPID assignment
MODULE 03 · VENDOR ~ 2s
Homewood is looked up in the CPID master (28k+ rows, synced daily at 19:00 from CieTrade's VM_Extract). The matching engine runs a weighted cascade:
  • Fuzzy name match — Levenshtein + token-set ratio returns a single strong candidate.
  • Remit-To address match — invoice's remit address hits an IsRemit=1 row for CPID HOMEWOOD001. This is the "50 Waste Managements" problem solved by address-aware indexing.
  • Stream-history tiebreak — Ops AI's StreamMatchingService confirms prior Rice Baking ↔ Homewood B/S history.
Composite confidence 0.97 ≥ 0.95 threshold → auto-assigns CPID. Payment method, terms, Sage #, and tax code auto-populate from the master.

Module 6

Four Special Workflow Branches

Not every invoice fits the standard pipeline. These four branches are first-class citizens in the state machine.

CM
Credit Memo
Bypasses B/S matching. Goes to dedicated queue with contra-offset linkage display ("picked up on check run X, offsetting A, B, C"). Manual terminal states only.
CI
Confidential Invoice
Separate mailbox, separate Graph subscription. Sensitivity-classified (High / Low). Group-based access enforced at DB + API + URL. Direct-to-Sage posting path.
SR
Statement Reconciliation
Vendor statements ingested as first-class docs. Reconciles against local ledger. Detects status mismatch, amount discrepancy, missing invoices — row by row.
CP
End-of-Month Create Pays
CP-terms counterparties. Batch upload on business day 10–11 (CSV/XLSX manifest + PDFs). Net positive → AP invoice; net negative → flip to AR. Atomic batch post.
