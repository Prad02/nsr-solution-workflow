Current State · As-Is Process Map

How NSR actually runs AP today, end to end.

A walk through the real, current process — what NSR does as a business, the systems they depend on, how data physically moves between those systems via SFTP files, and the 14 steps every invoice goes through from arrival to payment confirmation. Read this if you want to understand the "before" picture before looking at the SOW for what gets built next.

Volume: ~9,000 invoices per month (450/day)  ·  Currencies: USD + CAD  ·  Legal entity: Northstar Recycling Company, LLC
01 · The Business

NSR is a recycling company. Every transaction is a Buy/Sell.

NSR sources recyclable material from suppliers, moves it via haulers, and sells it to outlets. A single load of material moving from supplier to outlet is recorded in CieTrade as one Buy/Sell (B/S). Every cost, revenue, ledger, invoice, and payment in NSR's books traces back to a B/S.

B/S

The Buy/Sell record

Every load is captured in CieTrade as one Buy/Sell record. From that single record, CieTrade generates per-party ledgers (one for the supplier, one for the hauler, one for the outlet). Ledgers later get aggregated into invoices — sometimes one B/S becomes one invoice, sometimes many B/Ss roll up into one invoice line. That N:M relationship is the core matching complexity AP has to handle.

02 · The Systems

Five platforms, none of which talk to each other directly.

NSR's AP runs on top of five systems with very specific roles. They're connected through SFTP file drops, not APIs. Knowing what each one does is the first step to understanding why a problem in one place creates work in another.

ERP · Source of truth
CieTrade
"CTrade"
The ERP NSR runs the business on. Every Buy/Sell, counterparty, order, ledger, and invoice is created here. CieTrade is also where AP staff post invoices and where payment status comes back. If the data isn't in CieTrade, it doesn't exist for accounting.
Accounting · Books
Sage MAS 500
accounting platform
The accounting system. Every dollar in and out lands here. Year-end taxes, audit trail, reconciliation. Today it's heavily manual — accountants verify postings against bank activity. This is the system the new automation is meant to feed cleanly.
Automation · Today
HighRadius
"iRadius" · "IRDS"
Sits between CieTrade and Sage. Reads payment data and posting files from SFTP, matches payments to invoices, writes status back. The new platform replaces HighRadius's role on the AP side.
Doc storage · Legacy
Docstar / Eclipse
virtual document storage
The current AP document store. Every incoming invoice gets manually downloaded, sorted, coded, and uploaded here. Retired in the new platform — perpetual license, no migration of historical records.
Banking
JPMorgan Chase
"JPMC" · the bank
NSR's bank. Drops customer remittance into SFTP as lockbox image ZIPs and EDI 820 wire/ACH notifications. Used on the AR side (Phase 2 in the SOW). Phase 1 doesn't touch this; Phase 2 does.
03 · The Plumbing

Everything moves through SFTP — three folders, scheduled file drops.

CieTrade and Sage do not talk directly. CieTrade and HighRadius do not have APIs to one another. The entire integration is shared SFTP folders, where one system writes a flat file and the other one reads it on a schedule. Three folders carry the entire AP and AR data flow.

CTR → IRDS
/posting/outbound/
CieTrade writes · HighRadius reads
Posting batches. Pipe-delimited CSVs. Twice daily. Filename pattern Posting_NSR_MMDD.txt. Four key fields per row: UUID, AP_Batch_ID, Invoice_Number, CPID.
JPMC → IRDS
/lockbox/
JPMorgan Chase writes · HighRadius reads
Customer remittance data: lockbox image ZIPs (TIFF) and EDI 820 X12 v4010 (80-byte wrapped). AR-side, Phase 2 of the new build.
IRDS → CTR
/posting/inbound/
HighRadius writes · CieTrade reads
Payment status responses. PaymentResponse (POSTED or FAILED) and PaymentUpdate (-BA ACH, -CK check, -EF EFT). Daily ~09:00.
04 · The Flow

Fourteen steps from invoice arrival to "Paid".

This is the actual end-to-end path of an AP invoice today. Numbered, step-by-step, including who does what, which system holds the data, and where things break (the red callouts on selected steps).

1
Vendor → NSR
Vendor sends an invoice
A vendor (supplier, hauler, or service provider like Homewood Disposal) sends an invoice. It arrives via one of three channels: email to APayable@nsrecycle.com, an Account Manager forwarding from their own inbox, or it sits in a vendor portal (Waste Management, Waste Connections, GFL).
2
AP staff
AP triages the inbox
An AP coordinator opens the AP mailbox, identifies the invoice (versus general communication that also lands in this inbox), and downloads the attachment. For portal invoices, the coordinator logs into each portal manually and downloads PDFs one at a time.
Pain: ~9,000 invoices/month across three portals plus email plus AM forwards. Triage alone is full-time work for multiple people. Portals only retain ~2 years of history, so anything missed eventually disappears.
3
Docstar / Eclipse
Upload to virtual document storage
The AP coordinator uploads the PDF to Docstar (the current document management system) and assigns it to an AP coordinator for processing. The document gets a basic tag (Invoice, Credit Memo, Statement) and is filed.
Pain: Docstar is a separate UI from CieTrade. Linking a Docstar document to a CieTrade transaction requires a human looking at both screens.
4
AP staff
Read and code the invoice manually
The AP coordinator reads each field off the PDF and types it into a spreadsheet or directly into CieTrade. Vendor name, invoice number, dates, amounts, taxes, line items. Roughly 27 header fields and 5 fields per line item.
Pain: Hand transcription is the single biggest source of errors and the biggest time sink in the whole flow.
5
AP staff
Look up the vendor in CieTrade (CPID)
The coordinator searches CieTrade's counterparty master for the vendor. Vendor names rarely match perfectly between the invoice and the master. "Waste Management Inc." on the invoice might be one of fifty Waste Management entries with different addresses. The remit-to address often disambiguates.
Pain: Some vendors have 50+ CPID variants. Picking the wrong one breaks payment routing later.
6
AP staff & AM
Find the right Buy/Sell record
Now the coordinator searches CieTrade for the matching Buy/Sell. If the invoice has a B/S number on it, easy. If not, they search by vendor + ship date + amount, or by site + product, often relying on tribal knowledge or asking the Account Manager for help.
Pain: No B/S number on invoice happens often. Recurring B/Ss don't get created until the first business day of the month, so invoices sometimes arrive before their B/S exists at all.
7
AP staff
Match invoice lines to B/S lines (in a spreadsheet)
Three patterns of difficulty: (1) one B/S, one invoice, line-for-line — easy. (2) Multiple B/Ss, multiple invoice lines, each mapping cleanly — manageable. (3) One invoice line covering several B/Ss summed at the product level over a date range — this is the hard one. The coordinator has to enumerate candidate B/Ss in the period and find the subset whose total matches.
Pain: The hard case (level 3) takes hours per invoice. HighRadius cannot handle it. Spreadsheets are how it gets done today.
8
AP staff
Run validation checks (manually)
Eleven things to check before posting. Tax codes match. Ship dates within ±1 day. Bill-To address points to NSR's correct entity. Total balances. Tax type (GST/HST/QST) is correct for the vendor. Most of these are eyeball checks against a screen full of fields.
Pain: Anything missed shows up as a posting failure days later, and you can't tell which check it failed without rerunning the whole thing.
9
AM & AP via email
Resolve exceptions over email
If something doesn't match — price variance, weight mismatch, no B/S found, ship-date off — the coordinator emails the Account Manager. The AM looks into it, replies, sometimes pings the vendor. The thread stays in Outlook. The invoice waits in Docstar with no system-level state.
Pain: Invoices sit unresolved for days. There's no aging visibility, no SLA, no system of record. The "over 10 days outstanding" metric is generated by hand from spreadsheets.
10
CieTrade
Post the invoice in CieTrade
Once everything reconciles, the coordinator selects the matched ledgers in CieTrade, batches them under the invoice number, and clicks Post. CieTrade assigns an AP_Batch_ID — the same value as the invoice number, by convention.
11
CieTrade → SFTP
CieTrade writes a posting CSV
Twice a day, CieTrade serializes posted invoices into a pipe-delimited file: Posting_NSR_MMDD.txt. Four key fields per row: UUID, AP_Batch_ID, Invoice_Number, CPID. The file lands in the CTR → IRDS SFTP folder.
Pain: Only four fields in the file. Mismatches on three of those (everything except UUID) are a leading cause of HighRadius failing to auto-post.
12
HighRadius
HighRadius reads the file and tries to match
HighRadius pulls the posting file off the SFTP, validates each row, and tries to match it against the bank payment data it already has. When it works, it stamps the invoice POSTED. When it fails, the invoice gets dropped into a manual queue inside HighRadius for an accountant to inspect.
Pain: HighRadius can't handle the level-3 grouped invoices. Those routinely fall to manual.
13
HighRadius → SFTP
HighRadius writes payment status back
Daily around 09:00, HighRadius writes two file types into the IRDS → CTR folder. PaymentResponse reports POSTED or FAILED for each posted row. PaymentUpdate reports actual payment events with reference patterns: -BA for ACH, -CK for check, -EF for EFT.
14
CieTrade & Sage
CieTrade updates state · Sage gets the books
CieTrade reads the response files, updates each invoice's status (Posted → Paid as PaymentUpdate arrives), and surfaces the result in the AP screens. Posted invoices flow to Sage MAS 500 via a separate SFTP feed for the books. Sage may also have manual journal entries layered on top by accountants.
Pain: "Did we pay this?" remains a phone call between AP and Treasury. The answer lives across CieTrade, HighRadius, and Sage.

06 · Where It Breaks

The seven pain points the new platform exists to solve.

These are the things that turn a 75-minute clean-case invoice into a multi-day exception, or worse, a missed payment.

Pain 01
Manual triage at scale
9,000 invoices a month is too many for human triage. Multiple people do nothing but download, sort, and code. Portals make it worse — three different UIs, retention windows that drop history after about two years, plus all the AM-forwarded duplicates.
Pain 02
Vendor identification is fuzzy
Vendor names on invoices rarely match CPID master entries cleanly. "Waste Management" can resolve to fifty different CPIDs based on remit-to address. Pick the wrong one and the payment goes nowhere useful.
Pain 03
Buy/Sell matching is tribal
Without a B/S number on the invoice, the right match depends on knowing the vendor, the site, the date pattern, and the amount range. AP relies on Account Managers and institutional memory. New hires take months to be useful.
Pain 04
Level-3 grouped invoices break the system
When one invoice line aggregates many B/Ss across a date range, HighRadius cannot auto-match. These fall to manual every time, and they're the ones that take hours of spreadsheet work to reconcile.
Pain 05
No system of record for exceptions
Exception resolution lives in Outlook. AP emails AM, AM emails vendor, vendor emails back. The invoice has no system-level status during this. Aging is calculated by hand. Over-10-day reports are stitched together monthly from spreadsheets.
Pain 06
No audit trail
No reliable record of who touched an invoice, when, or what they changed. When something goes wrong months later, reconstructing the history is detective work across email threads, Docstar revisions, and CieTrade audit logs that don't link to invoice IDs.
Pain 07
"Did we pay this?" requires three systems
Posted state lives in CieTrade. Bank confirmation lives in HighRadius. Books reconciliation lives in Sage. Answering a vendor's payment-status email means logging into all three and stitching the answer together by hand.
