Tricky AP · Three totals on one invoice
Republic 2503-SHELBY · 3 service locations · $574.35 consolidated invoice total

Why this example

Republic Services rolls multiple service locations into a single consolidated invoice. This one (2503-SHELBY) covers three different 4-yard trash dumpsters at three South Post Rd addresses in Shelby NC. Each location prints its own "TOTAL $191.45" subtotal, plus the document has an "INVOICE AMOUNT $574.35" at the top. A naïve OCR extractor that grabs the first "TOTAL" it sees would underpay by $382.90.

This walkthrough uses the same pipeline as the Happy Path, but Stage 2 (OCR field-prioritization) does the real work — it picks the consolidated INVOICE AMOUNT, not any single location's TOTAL.

Why this example

Malvaes Solutions has a two-direction relationship with NSR. This invoice (M22278) is the AP haul direction — they pulled a trailer for us and charged $250 haul + $18.90 disposal of 0.14 lbs furniture/wood-fiber waste = $268.90 AP. A statement is a list of invoices, not an invoice. Posting it like AP would double-count every line. The classifier catches this at Module 2 and re-routes — no B/S match, no validation, no posting. Each row is matched against the local AP ledger; discrepancies surface as reconciliation tasks.

Why this example

Hard-stop exception · Vendor not found
CPID lookup miss · queue · human assigns · feedback loop

Why this example

Module 3 is where most exceptions are born. The CPID master holds 28k+ vendor rows, but new vendors arrive every week, and existing vendors mutate (new remit address, name change, acquisition). When the matching cascade can't reach 0.95 confidence on any candidate, the invoice hard-stops into the Vendor Not Found queue — never B/S match, never validation, never posting.

The interesting part isn't the stop, it's the learning loop: whatever the human picks (assign existing CPID, create a new one, void as not-an-invoice) feeds back into the matcher. Next time this vendor sends an invoice, it auto-resolves.

Three write-backs happen atomically:
